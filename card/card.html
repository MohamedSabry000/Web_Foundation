  • Sleep: Rabbits may appear to be crepuscular—most active at dawn and at dusk—but their natural inclination is toward nocturnal activity.

  • Habitat and range: Rabbit habitats include meadows, woods, forests, grasslands, deserts and wetlands. Rabbits live in groups, and the best known species, the European rabbit, lives in underground burrows, or rabbit holes. A group of burrows is called a warren.

Rabbits are small mammals in the family Leporidae of the order Lagomorpha. Oryctolagus cuniculus includes the European rabbit species and its descendants, the over 200 breeds of domestic rabbit.
